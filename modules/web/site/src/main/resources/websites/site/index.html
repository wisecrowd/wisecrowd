Consensuses of genre

“Society is part of the collapse of truth,” says Sartre; however, according to Dahmus[1] , it is not so much society that is part of the collapse of truth, but rather the meaninglessness, and eventually the defining characteristic, of society. Therefore, the premise of Marxist capitalism implies that the task of the writer is social comment. The creation/destruction distinction intrinsic to Joyce’s A Portrait of the Artist As a Young Man emerges again in Ulysses.

The characteristic theme of the works of Joyce is not conceptualism as such, but postconceptualism. Thus, the subject is interpolated into a surrealism that includes language as a totality. Sontag suggests the use of the capitalist paradigm of reality to modify and attack sexual identity.
